Research Methodology & Provenance
Sources Vignelli's "severe" palette of red, black, and white is well-documented across scholarship on his work, including the Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum archives and the Vignelli Center for Design Studies at RIT (Rochester, NY), where the Massimo and Lella Vignelli papers are held. The Vignelli Center's own Tumblr archive (2014–2017) confirms that Pantone Super Warm Red — catalogued in Box 506 of the Vignelli papers — is the authoritative reference for "Vignelli Red." The digital equivalent of Pantone Warm Red C is universally cited as #F9423A / RGB(249, 66, 58). Vignelli himself described his red as "somewhere on the border of red and orange," which aligns precisely with this slightly red-orange Pantone value. The secondary colors (yellow, blue) are inferred from his documented work on the NYC Subway map (1972) and other projects; exact Pantone specifications for these are noted as approximations. Warm gray derives from his frequent use of Pantone Warm Gray series in print work.
