Research Methodology & Notes
Sources & Confidence The canonical color list for Hellerware — pink, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple, and white — is confirmed by the Vignelli Center for Design Studies archival brochure documented on Google Arts & Culture, which reproduces the original Heller Rainbow Dinnerware brochure designed by Massimo Vignelli. The dinnerware was originally produced in white only (1964, Italian manufacture), then yellow was added when Heller introduced the line in the US (1971). The full rainbow set was expanded in the mid-1970s.

Because Heller never published official Pantone specifications for these production colors, all RGB and hex values here are visual approximations derived from multiple product photographs — including MoMA Design Store listings, Tenon Design vintage inventory, and Remodelista editorial photography — and cross-referenced against Pantone C coated equivalents. They should be treated as close working values, not archivally precise specifications. For print production, physical color matching against original dinnerware is recommended. Note that the Hellerware Blue (#2060A8) is a warmer cobalt, distinct from the deeper Reflex Blue used in Vignelli's graphic work.

Additionally, later production runs (1980s–present) include red and black, which are already represented in the primary Vignelli palette and are therefore not duplicated here.
